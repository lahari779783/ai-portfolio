ENGINEERING DECISIONS

Architectural choices focused on workflow reliability, operational simplicity, and low-latency request handling.

Why Async?

Prevent AI latency from blocking synchronous API response handling during emergency processing.

Why Redis?

Lightweight queue coordination with low operational complexity for current infrastructure scale.

Why FSM?

Preserve workflow consistency by blocking invalid transitions during rerouting and retries.

Why Fallback Routing?

Emergency workflows cannot pause during AI or provider failures, requiring continuity guarantees.

EMERGENCY COORDINATION VISION

Rethinking emergency response beyond traditional ambulance-first workflows.

The Problem With Traditional Emergency Flow

In many emergencies, families do not wait for an ambulance first.

They immediately begin moving toward the hospital using their own vehicle to save time.

  • traffic systems are unaware
  • nearby drivers cannot identify the emergency
  • hospitals are unprepared
  • ambulances operate independently
  • coordination becomes fragmented

Critical minutes are lost during communication and routing.

MedRoute’s Coordination Model

Instead of waiting for emergency systems sequentially, MedRoute explores a coordinated movement model.

  • ambulance dispatch occurs simultaneously
  • hospitals prepare in advance
  • routing intelligence continuously adapts
  • emergency coordination remains active throughout the journey
